Milan: A Day of Shopping

02.05.2019

A look into a day in Milan from the perspective of muah. From shopping 'til I dropped to going to see beautiful art to wandering the streets around the Milan Duomo, my day in Milan was, well, amazing.

Nothing and I mean nothing, comes close to my passion for moda, or fashion. I’ve worshipped fashion magazines since I was little, and even when I used to read the tween magazines like J-14, I would immediately flip to the red carpet looks and write blurbs on the side of each outfit of what I liked and hated about them (like someone should really recruit me for Fashion Police because I’m a brutally honest critic.) So to cure my need for retail therapy, I took a solo train day trip to Milan this past Saturday, June 16. My first order of business that day was to look fashionable because I refused to be seen in Milan looking like trash even if I did have to get up early that morning to catch my train. So, I threw on my go-to white tee, a cute black print scarf that I borrowed from my mama and comfy, yet cute Levi’s and my go-to sneaks. And just like that, I was out the door in a brisk walk to my platform.

The top of my list: the Milano Cathedral, or Duomo di Milano. And boy, oh boy was she a beaut. I felt like crying, and in fact, I did tear up (don’t judge me, I’m an architectural and design nerd). If you go to Italy and don’t have a deeper appreciation for ornate and PHENOMENAL DOORS afterward, then honestly we can’t be friends because the doors put me in awe of the people that get to live there every day. Honestly, if an Italian family called me up and said, “Hey Lauren, we want to adopt you. Like you can keep your American family, but come live in our Tuscan villa with cool doors for a little bit of money!” I’d be out – a one-way ticket to Italy here I come. Again, don’t judge me. My point is the Milan Cathedral bronze doors were amazing. My next order of business was to walk through the buildings and just wander a bit. Milan is like a way better European New York City, so explore. I found a cute little

coffee shop and a cute pizza place that I ate lunch at. Then, I headed toward the fashion district and the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele, where all of the designer stores are and the architecture is amazing. The Galleria is the oldest mall in the world, and it’s breathtaking (it’s also a great place to get shade in the summer because it’s covered). I did a lot of shopping in this area, like a ridiculous amount. Then, I got a Coke slushie to freshen up after I had some pizza and headed to a design museum I had done some research on previously: La Triennale di Milano Museum. The museum was awesome and truly had something for everyone. I enjoyed the furniture design exhibit, the interactive exhibits and their curation of Italian Instagrammers by color and location. The museum cafe is amazing, too. I treated myself to a coffee and a Sicilian cannoli.
